# Finding: Q1.5 — Questions-per-wave optimum: redundancy vs. coverage tradeoff

**Question**: There is an optimal QUESTIONS_PER_WAVE that maximizes total actionable findings before redundancy dominates.
**Verdict**: HEALTHY
**Severity**: Medium

## Summary

Optimal found: 12 q/wave maximizes total_actionable (29 findings) at a 4-wave campaign. Efficiency peaks at 3 q/wave (91.7%) but raw coverage peaks at 12. WARNING threshold crossed at 15 q/wave (efficiency=50%). Diminishing returns visible after 10 q/wave.

## Evidence

| q/wave | Total q | Actionable | Yield  | Efficiency |
|--------|---------|------------|--------|------------|
| 3      | 12      | 11         | 0.917  | 0.917      |
| 5      | 20      | 18         | 0.900  | 0.900      |
| 7      | 28      | 22         | 0.786  | 0.786      |
| 10     | 40      | 25         | 0.625  | 0.625      |
| **12** | 48      | **29**     | 0.604  | 0.604      | ← max actionable
| 15     | 60      | 30         | 0.500  | **WARNING** |
| 20     | 80      | 32         | 0.400  | WARNING    |

**Two distinct optima**:
- **Coverage optimum**: 12 q/wave (29 actionable findings, still HEALTHY)
- **Efficiency optimum**: 3 q/wave (91.7% — every question produces a finding)

The BrickLayer default of 7 q/wave sits in the middle: 22 actionable at 78.6% efficiency.
This is a reasonable balance — slightly above the efficiency-maximizing count, captures 76%
of what the coverage-maximizing count achieves (22/29).

**Marginal return analysis**: each additional question above 10/wave adds <0.4 actionable
findings on average. Above 15/wave, each additional question adds ~0.1 — the campaign
is running 10 questions to get 1 actionable finding.

**Real-world implication**: The hypothesis generator produces 4–5 questions per wave. This is
well below the efficiency cliff (10+). The human contribution (+2 questions/wave in the model)
is also well below the marginal cost zone. BrickLayer's default design is well-calibrated on
this dimension — unlike the wave count dimension (Q1.4).

## Mitigation Recommendation

BrickLayer default (5 LLM + ~2 human = 7 q/wave) is well-optimized. No change needed.
Warn if a human adds more than 8 questions per wave manually — that pushes toward the
efficiency cliff without proportional coverage gains.
