# Finding: Q1.2 — Novelty cliff: find the DOMAIN_NOVELTY threshold that crosses WARNING

**Question**: There is a specific DOMAIN_NOVELTY value (estimated 0.60–0.75) where campaign_yield crosses below WARNING even with full peer review.
**Verdict**: FAILURE
**Severity**: High

## Summary

No novelty cliff exists with full peer review. At DOMAIN_NOVELTY=1.0 + PEER_REVIEW_RATE=1.0, yield=0.643 — still HEALTHY. Peer review is over-correcting; the model is miscalibrated for frontier domains.

## Evidence

With PEER_REVIEW_RATE=1.0, yield never crosses the WARNING threshold (0.45) across the full
DOMAIN_NOVELTY range 0.50–1.00:

| Novelty | Yield | Verdict |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 0.50    | 0.786 | HEALTHY |
| 0.70    | 0.750 | HEALTHY |
| 0.90    | 0.714 | HEALTHY |
| 1.00    | 0.643 | HEALTHY |  ← peer review saving a 100% novel domain

Without peer review (PEER_REVIEW_RATE=0.0), the crossing appears between 0.85 and 0.90:

| Novelty | Yield | Verdict |
|---------|-------|---------|
| 0.85    | 0.571 | HEALTHY |
| 0.90    | 0.464 | WARNING |  ← crossing ~0.87
| 1.00    | 0.429 | WARNING |

**Root cause**: The peer review correction formula applies PEER_REVIEW_CORRECTION_RATE=0.55 even
at extreme novelty, after only a novelty_discount of max(0.20, 1 - 0.90*0.60) = 0.46. At
DOMAIN_NOVELTY=1.0, peer correction = 1.0 * drift_rate * 0.55 * max(0.20, 0.40) = drift * 0.22.

With a large enough drift_rate, this correction still adds significant yield back. The model
assumes peer review can correct 22% of drifted verdicts even in a totally unknown domain.
In practice, if the primary agent cannot assess a frontier domain, neither can the peer reviewer
— they have the same training distribution. The correction should approach zero at extreme novelty.

## Mitigation Recommendation

Recalibrate constants.py and `_peer_correction()` in simulate.py:

1. Change `PEER_REVIEW_CORRECTION_RATE` from 0.55 to 0.40 (peer review is less effective than modeled)
2. In `_peer_correction()`, change novelty discount formula from:
   `max(0.20, 1.0 - DOMAIN_NOVELTY * 0.60)` to `max(0.05, 1.0 - DOMAIN_NOVELTY * 0.90)`
   This makes correction approach 5% (near-zero) at high novelty instead of 20%.

With this fix, the novelty cliff should appear around DOMAIN_NOVELTY=0.65–0.75 — consistent with
empirical expectation that campaigns become unreliable well before the theoretical maximum novelty.
