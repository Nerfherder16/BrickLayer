## Interpretation

### Why the cliff moved right

The three recalibrations collectively reduced the effective accuracy per-question. With lower BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY (0.50 vs 0.625) and lower PEER_REVIEW_CORRECTION_RATE (0.40 vs 0.55), the net effective_accuracy at nominal parameters dropped from the prior baseline, but the absolute level (~0.79 at DN=0.35, ASR=0.65) is still high enough that the yield is dominated by the **wave uniqueness decay** (saturation), not accuracy failures.

The novelty cliff only manifests when the combined novelty_penalty + reduced peer_correction drop effective_accuracy below the level where the uniqueness decay also kicks in hard. With the recalibrated parameters, that crossing happens at DN≈0.95, not the 0.60–0.80 range that Q6.1 predicted.

**Root cause**: The recalibrations lowered accuracy, but the dominant factor in the 4-wave yield is still `_wave_uniqueness()` saturation (Wave 4 uniqueness=0.55 causes the yield to drop to 0.429 even at nominal). The accuracy penalty needs to be much more severe (extreme novelty) before it becomes the limiting factor. This is a model calibration insight: at DN below ~0.90, saturation governs; at DN above 0.90, accuracy collapse governs.

### Why no specialization floor was found

With RECALIBRATED_BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY=0.50, even a fully-generalist fleet at the recalibrated DN=0.35 achieves effective_accuracy ~(0.50 * novelty_mult + peer_correction). The peer correction at low novelty remains meaningful (novelty_discount=0.685 at DN=0.35), and the effective accuracy stays well above 0.60, producing yield around 0.643. The 0.45 threshold is simply not reached before ASR=0.00.

This suggests the specialization ratio has less leverage on campaign yield than originally modeled — or that the warning threshold of 0.45 is too aggressive relative to where the recalibrated model actually fails. A threshold of 0.60 would show the floor at ASR≈0.20.

The cliff and floor displacement suggests the recalibrated model is more resilient than Q6.1 predicted across the DN=[0.55–0.85] range, but this resilience may be an artifact of saturation dominating the signal. The model should be tested with higher WAVE_COUNT (≥6) and larger QUESTIONS_PER_WAVE to force saturation earlier and expose the accuracy-driven collapse at lower DN values. Q7.2+ should test these extended configurations.
