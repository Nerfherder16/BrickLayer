## Interpretation

No variant achieves HEALTHY verdict at WAVE_COUNT=20. The failures are structural, not parametric:

1. **Wave novelty floor = 0.000 is deterministic given seed=42**: Wave 2 has uniqueness=0.20, and the RNG produces 7 consecutive misses. This is seed-specific but the floor gate `WAVE_NOVELTY_FLOOR_FAILURE=0.15` has no tolerance for even one zero-yield wave. The floor constraint is too strict for the stochastic simulation at low-uniqueness waves.

2. **Synthesis coherence < 0.35 is structural at WAVE_COUNT=20**: The coherence model applies per-wave redundancy pressure of 0.12/wave, which means by wave 12 the multiplier is max(0.30, 1-11×0.12) = 0.30, compressing yields severely. At 20 waves, coherence is mathematically constrained below 0.35 unless yields are very high in late waves.

3. **Phase 2 mean improvement is real but insufficient**: Variant B raises Phase 2 from 0.339 (baseline) to 0.464 — a +0.125 gain — and passes the ±0.20 tolerance check against 0.642. But the 0.50 gate is not met (shortfall: 0.036).

**Root cause of Phase 2 shortfall**: The stochastic gate at wave 11 (uniqueness=0.613, yield=0.000) is the decisive failure. With a different seed, Variant B would likely clear 0.50. The simulation's stochasticity at 7 q/wave is high enough that a single RNG run is not a reliable discriminant.

**Implication**: The 0.50 Phase 2 gate is approximately correct as a structural target. Variant B's compressed-window approach is directionally right — the Phase 2 improvement of +0.125 over baseline confirms the root cause diagnosis from Q7.2 (rise phase too slow). However, the combination of the wave_novelty_floor gate and synthesis_coherence gate make HEALTHY structurally unreachable at WAVE_COUNT=20 under the current constants, regardless of the J-curve shape.

**Recommended path**: The Q8.1 failure is driven by two secondary gates (floor + coherence) rather than the uniqueness model per se. Q8 should test whether WAVE_COUNT=15 (avoiding the coherence compression wall) allows Variant B to reach HEALTHY. Variant B — compressed window (waves 8–12) — is the recommended J-curve recalibration: it achieves the best Phase 2 result (0.464, passes ±0.20 tolerance), has the highest campaign yield among variants with Phase 1 and Phase 3 also within tolerance, and its structural logic (fast ramp to plateau) is sound.
