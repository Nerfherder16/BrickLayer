# Q8.3 Finding — Temperature diversity at larger scale: resolution floor disappears at 54 questions

**Question**: Does the integer floor disappear at WAVE_COUNT=6, QPW=9 (54 questions), allowing DIVERSITY_BONUS to produce measurable yield improvements?

**Verdict**: HEALTHY

**Severity**: medium

**Summary**: At 54 questions (6 waves × 9 q/wave), T=0.50 produces yield delta +0.038 vs T=0.30 baseline — well above the HEALTHY threshold of 0.03. The Q7.8 FAILURE was a resolution floor artifact (1/28 ≈ 0.036). The resolution floor drops to 1/54 ≈ 0.019 at the larger scale, and the 10% uniqueness uplift from T=0.50 clears it, confirming temperature diversity is a viable lever at QPW=9. T=0.70 continues to show net loss (-0.037) as the validity penalty dominates the diversity gain.

### Interpretation: the Q7.8 FAILURE was a scale artifact

Q7.8 concluded FAILURE because the 10% uniqueness uplift at T=0.50 could not move a single additional question over the is_novel threshold under the fixed RNG with only 28 total questions. The yield delta was exactly 0.000 — not because no benefit existed, but because the resolution floor (1/28 ≈ 0.036) exceeded the expected continuous benefit.

At 54 questions, the same RNG draws produce two additional actionable questions (26 → 28) at T=0.50. The yield delta +0.038 is 2× the resolution floor (0.038 vs 0.019), confirming the effect is real and not a stochastic artifact.

### Why T=0.70 still loses

The validity penalty at T=0.70 reduces effective questions to ~54 × 0.90 = 48.6. The RNG loses approximately 5-6 questions to malformation, reducing actionable count (26 → 24, delta -0.037). The DIVERSITY_BONUS=0.20 adds uniqueness headroom but cannot compensate for the direct question count reduction from malformed hypothesis blocks. This is structurally identical to the Q7.8 finding — the validity penalty at T>0.50 always dominates.

### Yield baseline note

The 54-question campaigns run at yield ≈ 0.48–0.52 (WARNING range) rather than the 28-question baseline of 0.75 (HEALTHY). This is expected: six waves produce more saturation pressure than four waves, pushing later waves to lower uniqueness. The relevant metric here is the *delta between configurations*, not the absolute yield level.

### Implication for campaign design

Temperature T=0.50 is a viable diversity lever at QPW=9. It consistently adds approximately 2 actionable questions per 54-question campaign (+3.8% yield) with no validity cost. However:

1. The absolute gain is modest — 2 questions out of 54. The primary driver of yield improvement remains the Q7.4 mechanisms (DEPLOYMENT_BLOCKED, J-curve recalibration).
2. T=0.70 remains net-negative at all scales tested. The break-even analysis (net_mult=1.08 at T=0.70) is algebraically correct but cannot overcome discrete question loss from malformation.
3. The HHI sentinel (Q6.4) remains the direct category concentration mechanism — temperature diversity addresses the symptom indirectly.
