**Agent**: synthesizer-bl2
**Verdict**: FAILURE
**Severity**: High
**Confidence**: 0.95

## Implications

The four recalibrations interact destructively when combined at a 4-wave baseline:

1. **The inverted `_wave_uniqueness()` is incompatible with WAVE_COUNT=4.** Starting uniqueness at 0.20 means the first four waves are all in Phase 1 (low uniqueness). Wave 2 produces zero actionable findings by RNG chance at such low uniqueness, collapsing wave_novelty_floor to 0.000 and failing the secondary gate. The J-curve inversion only produces HEALTHY at WAVE_COUNT >= 15-20 where waves eventually reach the high-uniqueness plateau.

2. **Changes 1–3 compound too aggressively at 4 waves.** At WAVE_COUNT=4, there is no opportunity for later waves to compensate for early wave failures. The yield must be high in all waves; lowering effective accuracy and peer correction simultaneously prevents recovery.

3. **The "novelty cliff" and "specialization floor" hypotheses are untestable with this recalibration.** Both require a HEALTHY baseline to fall from. Since the model starts in WARNING, all parameter sweeps only move further into degraded territory — there is no cliff to observe, only a floor that was already below HEALTHY.

**Recommended path forward:**
- Apply recalibrations 1–3 independently from recalibration 4 (wave uniqueness inversion)
- Test the J-curve model separately at WAVE_COUNT=20–36 where it is validated
- For the 4-wave baseline, use a moderate-rise uniqueness (e.g., start=0.60, gentle rise) rather than full inversion
- The validated range for recalibrations 1–3 requires either increasing WAVE_COUNT (>=6–8) or softening one of the three changes to restore the HEALTHY baseline
