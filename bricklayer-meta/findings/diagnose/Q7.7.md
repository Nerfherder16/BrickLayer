# Q7.7 — SUBJECTIVE Verdict Rate Ceiling: 15% Rate Validation

**Status**: DONE
**Verdict**: FAILURE
**Severity**: HIGH
**Confidence**: 0.90

---

## Summary

The 15% SUBJECTIVE verdict ceiling recommended by Q6.7 **does NOT pass** the HEALTHY threshold under Model A. Under Model A (SUBJECTIVE findings count as 0.5 toward yield), 15% produces yield=0.694 (WARNING), falling 2.2 percentage points below the HEALTHY floor of 0.716. Only Model B (human-resolution queue, 70% resolved per wave) passes HEALTHY at 15% (yield=0.721). The ceiling is model-dependent and should be tightened.

---

## Test Configuration

- **Baseline**: yield=0.750, total_questions=28, total_actionable=21 (HEALTHY)
- **HEALTHY threshold**: 0.716 (95.4% of baseline, per Q6.7 method: 0.545/0.571 ratio)
- **Resolution rate (Model B)**: 70% of queued SUBJECTIVE findings resolved per wave
- **SUBJECTIVE rates tested**: 0%, 12%, 15%, 18%

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Model Asymmetry is Large and Operationally Significant

Model A and Model B diverge by 2.7 percentage points at 15% rate (0.694 vs 0.721). This gap exceeds the width of the HEALTHY/WARNING boundary for this campaign. The choice of accounting model is not a theoretical preference — it changes the verdict from WARNING to HEALTHY.

### Finding 2: 15% Ceiling Fails Under Model A — Only Model B Passes

The 15% ceiling recommended in Q6.7 was validated only at an implied Model A treatment (SUBJECTIVE counted as 0.5). Under Model A, even 12% falls below HEALTHY. The ceiling is only defensible if the team operates under Model B semantics (active human review queue with ≥70% wave resolution rate).

### Finding 3: Model B Has a Lower Effective Ceiling Too

At 18%, Model B also falls below HEALTHY (0.715 vs threshold 0.716 — margin of 0.001). The Model B ceiling is approximately 17-18%, not 20%+. Both models are sensitive to subjective rate in the 12-18% range.

### Finding 4: Human Review Burden Diverges Significantly by Model

- Model A at 15%: burden=3.15 findings permanently unresolved (no resolution pathway)
- Model B at 15%: burden=0.83 findings remaining after final wave (queue drains over time)

Model B not only produces better yield but also reduces the human review backlog through its resolution mechanism.

---

## Root Cause

The 15% ceiling was recommended in Q6.7 based on proportional delta analysis (midpoint between 10% and 20%) without testing the actual yield floor against the HEALTHY threshold. Under Model A accounting, any SUBJECTIVE rate above ~10% drops yield below HEALTHY. The ceiling needs to be model-conditional:

- **Model A ceiling**: ~8-10% (to stay above HEALTHY threshold of 0.716)
- **Model B ceiling**: ~15-17% (HEALTHY passes at 15%, WARNING at 18%)

---

## Recommendations

1. **Adopt Model B semantics** if a 15% ceiling is desired. This requires an active human review queue with ≥70% per-wave resolution commitment. If that cannot be guaranteed operationally, the ceiling must drop to ≤10%.

2. **Lower the Model-A ceiling to 10%** if human review throughput is uncertain. At 10%, Model A yield ≈ 0.713 (borderline HEALTHY); further testing at exactly 10% is recommended.

3. **Treat 15% as a hard cap, not a target**. SUBJECTIVE findings represent genuine ambiguity — optimizing toward the ceiling is counterproductive. A healthy campaign should trend toward 5-8%.

4. **Document the model assumption in the campaign brief**. Future campaigns should specify which accounting model applies before setting a SUBJECTIVE rate ceiling, as the verdict outcome depends entirely on this choice.
