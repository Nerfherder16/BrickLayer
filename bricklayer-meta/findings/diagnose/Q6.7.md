This effectively removes all generalist misses. A real runner contract tightens output format/structure, not accuracy; the accuracy effect would be more modest. The +0.215 should be treated as an upper bound for "perfect runner standardization."

## Interpretation

Change 1 (SUBJECTIVE) is expected to reduce yield — it is a quality/correctness feature, not a throughput feature. The -0.053 degradation at 20% is significant and warrants a rate ceiling: creative-domain campaigns should warn if SUBJECTIVE_RATE exceeds 0.15 in a wave, as the yield drop at 0.20 is roughly triple the drop at 0.10.

Change 2 (runner contract) dominates all other changes in this model (+0.215 upper bound), but the actual effect depends on how much generalist variance is driving missed findings in real campaigns. The model prescribes this as the highest-priority change, but empirical measurement is needed before accepting the magnitude.

Change 3 (pluggable evaluate.py) produces a robust +0.108 delta by raising AGENT_SPECIALIZATION_RATIO from 0.65 to 0.82 — a 26% relative increase in specialist coverage. This is a concrete, measurable roadmap improvement with a clear mechanism: more questions get specialists rather than generalists.

Change 4 (deployment split) yields a small but consistent gain (+0.035-0.037) with negligible model uncertainty. The Q6.3 mechanism (DEPLOYMENT_BLOCKED status) directly implements this change and has already been specified.
