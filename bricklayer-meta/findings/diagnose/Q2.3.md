# Finding: Q2.3 — Peer review consistency: do reviewed findings show different verdicts?

**Question**: Are findings with peer review sections more likely to be WARNING/FAILURE? Does peer review preferentially target high-signal findings, or is it applied uniformly?
**Agent**: regulatory-researcher
**Verdict**: WARNING
**Severity**: Medium

## Summary

Peer-reviewed findings have a 94.7% signal rate vs 56.7% for unreviewed — a massive gap. BUT: all 23 peer-reviewed findings are from waves 13–16, which are the highest-signal waves in the campaign. Peer review is NOT applied uniformly; it was active only in a concentrated cluster of waves. The apparent signal boost is a wave confound, not a genuine peer review effect.

## Evidence

### Verdict Distribution

| Group | HEALTHY | WARNING | FAILURE | Other | Total | Signal Rate |
|-------|---------|---------|---------|-------|-------|-------------|
| With Peer Review | 1 | 7 | 11 | 4 | 23 | **94.7%** |
| Without Peer Review | 58 | 34 | 42 | 50 | 184 | **56.7%** |

### Wave Distribution of Peer Review

All 23 peer-reviewed findings come from waves 13–16:
- Wave 13: Q13.1–Q13.8 + follow-up variants (13 findings)
- Wave 14: Q14.4 (1 finding)
- Wave 15: Q15.1–Q15.5 (5 findings)
- Wave 16: Q16.3–Q16.4 (2 findings)
- Waves 17–36: **0 peer-reviewed findings**

### Confound: Wave 13 Is the Highest-Signal Wave

Wave 13 alone (83.3% signal rate) accounts for most of the peer-reviewed sample. These findings would have high signal WITH or WITHOUT peer review. The peer review appearance is not causing the high signal rate — both peer review and high signal are effects of the same cause: wave 13 was a uniquely productive wave.

### What CONFIRMED/CONCERNS/OVERRIDE Verdicts Look Like

The 23 peer-reviewed findings show:
- 2 with verdict "CONFIRMED" (Q15.3, Q15.4) — unusual verdicts, may be peer review verdicts overwriting finding verdicts
- 1 with verdict "CONCERNS" (Q15.5) — similarly unusual
- These suggest formatting inconsistency: the peer review verdict was written into the finding's verdict field instead of a separate subsection

## Verdict Rationale

WARNING because:
1. Peer review was applied for only ~4 waves out of 36 (waves 13-16), then appears to have stopped
2. The inconsistent verdict formats (CONFIRMED/CONCERNS appearing as finding verdicts) suggest the peer review integration is fragile
3. With no peer review in waves 17-36 (including 20+ waves of high-signal findings), the system is operating without a key quality gate

## Mitigation Recommendation

1. **Investigate why peer review stopped after wave 16**: Was it disabled? Did the Forge decide it was no longer needed? Or did it simply fail silently?

2. **Fix the verdict format inconsistency**: CONFIRMED/CONCERNS/OVERRIDE should never appear in the finding's `**Verdict**:` field — those are peer review verdicts, not finding verdicts. The peer review system needs to write to `## Peer Review` without modifying the top-level verdict.

3. **Peer review coverage should be measured as a campaign health metric**: Coverage of <10% across 36 waves indicates the system is not functioning as designed.

## Open Follow-up Questions

- **Q2.3a**: What changed between wave 16 and wave 17 that caused peer review to stop? Is there a configuration change, a Forge sentinel, or a bug?
- **Q2.3b**: Would running peer review retrospectively on wave 24 findings (75% signal rate) change any verdicts, or would it all be CONFIRMED?
