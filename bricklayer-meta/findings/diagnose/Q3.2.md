# Finding: Q3.2 — Question bank saturation: how many unique failure categories appear?

**Question**: How many distinct failure categories appear in the Recall campaign findings? Does the campaign show broad coverage or concentrated clustering around a few failure modes?
**Agent**: competitive-analyst
**Verdict**: WARNING
**Severity**: Medium

## Summary

Only 9 distinct failure themes across 94 WARNING/FAILURE findings. Two categories dominate: retrieval (55.3%) and decay (27.7%) account for 83% of all failures. The campaign is heavily clustered, not broadly distributed. This is WARNING-level: the depth of coverage within these two failure modes is excellent, but the breadth is severely limited — the campaign may have missed entire failure categories it never probed.

### Coverage Distribution Index

A perfectly uniform coverage across 9 categories would be ~10.5 findings/category. Actual distribution:
- **Top 2 categories**: 78 findings (83% of total)
- **Bottom 7 categories**: 16 findings (17% of total)
- **Gini coefficient (estimated)**: ~0.75 (highly concentrated)

### Potential Missing Categories

Based on BrickLayer architecture and Recall system design, categories that were likely underexplored:
- **write path correctness**: memory storage failures, hash collisions, embedding failures
- **authentication/permissions**: multi-user isolation, session leakage
- **backup/disaster recovery**: what happens when Qdrant or Neo4j data is corrupted
- **rate limiting**: API abuse scenarios
- **graph correctness**: Neo4j causal edge integrity, cycle detection
- **cold-start behavior**: empty corpus performance, first-use experience

### Why Retrieval Dominates

The hypothesis generator correctly identified retrieval as the most user-visible failure mode (Q13.1: 94.6% of memories never injected). This CRITICAL FAILURE generated a cascade of follow-up questions across waves 13-36. The depth of retrieval coverage is appropriate — but it may have crowded out other failure categories.

### The Decay Cluster

The double-decay bug (Q22.1) generated 8+ re-check questions across waves 22-30 (see Q2.4). These deployment-verification questions inflated the "decay" category count without adding new diagnostic coverage. If re-checks are excluded, the decay cluster is ~8-10 genuine findings, not 26.

## Verdict Rationale

WARNING because:
1. 83% concentration in 2 themes suggests the hypothesis generator followed high-signal clusters but may have missed other failure domains
2. The simulation's coverage model assumes questions distribute across failure modes — reality shows the generator is attracted to failure clusters once they're identified
3. 6+ likely failure categories (write path, auth, backup, etc.) have near-zero coverage

## Mitigation Recommendation

1. **Add category diversity as a campaign health metric**: At each wave, if >60% of prior findings are in the same 2 categories, the hypothesis generator should be prompted to explore underexplored areas.

2. **The hypothesis generator needs a coverage map**: It should be fed the current finding distribution and explicitly asked to probe categories with <5% coverage.

3. **Distinguish re-checks from new findings in results.tsv**: Deployment-verification re-checks (like "is Q22.1 fixed?") should not count against the category distribution — they inflate the decay count without adding coverage.

4. **For BrickLayer evolution**: A general framework needs explicit coverage targets per failure mode category, not just aggregate yield metrics.
