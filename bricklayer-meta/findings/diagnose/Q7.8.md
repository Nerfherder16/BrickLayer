# Q7.8 Finding — Hypothesis temperature and category clustering

**Question**: Does raising HYPOTHESIS_TEMPERATURE reduce retrieval+decay concentration?

**Verdict**: FAILURE

**Severity**: medium

**Summary**: No temperature between 0.30 and 0.70 produces higher campaign yield than the default T=0.30. At T=0.50, the diversity bonus (+10% uniqueness uplift) is algebraically net-positive (net_mult=1.10) but produces zero measurable yield improvement in discrete simulation (yield delta=0.000). At T=0.70, the validity penalty dominates, producing a net yield loss of -0.107. Temperature increase is not a viable lever for reducing category concentration under the current 7 q/wave configuration.

### Break-even analysis

The break-even formula is: `DB_needed = (1.0 / validity) - 1.0`

For a temperature T to improve yield, the net multiplier `validity * (1 + DB_actual)` must exceed 1.0.

| Temperature | validity | DB_actual | DB_needed | net_mult | Status |
|-------------|----------|-----------|-----------|----------|--------|
| T=0.50      | 1.0000   | 0.1000    | 0.0000    | 1.1000   | ABOVE  |
| T=0.55      | 0.9750   | 0.1250    | 0.0256    | 1.0969   | ABOVE  |
| T=0.60      | 0.9500   | 0.1500    | 0.0526    | 1.0925   | ABOVE  |
| T=0.65      | 0.9250   | 0.1750    | 0.0811    | 1.0869   | ABOVE  |
| T=0.70      | 0.9000   | 0.2000    | 0.1111    | 1.0800   | ABOVE  |

All temperatures from 0.50 to 0.70 have net_mult > 1.0 — they are algebraically above break-even. However, the discrete simulation (integer question counts, fixed RNG seed) shows no measurable improvement at T=0.50 and a significant loss at T=0.70.

### Interpretation: the continuous/discrete discrepancy

The break-even analysis operates on continuous multipliers; the simulation operates on discrete question counts with a fixed RNG. At T=0.50, the 10% uniqueness uplift changes uniqueness from (e.g.) 0.70 → 0.77 per wave, but the same RNG draws produce the same is_novel/is_accurate outcomes for all 28 questions. The yield stays at 21/28 = 0.7500.

This reveals a **resolution floor**: with only 7 q/wave × 4 waves = 28 total questions, the minimum detectable yield improvement from a continuous uniqueness uplift is approximately 1/28 ≈ 0.036. A 10% uniqueness boost that translates to fractionally higher expected actionable count will not move the integer outcome under a fixed RNG — it requires the boost to push marginal questions over the is_novel threshold.

At T=0.70, the validity penalty removes ~10% of questions entirely (malformed gate), reducing effective inputs to ~25.2 out of 28. The RNG loses 2–3 questions to malformation, directly reducing total_actionable from 21 to 18 (yield 0.643).

### Why temperature is not a viable concentration lever

Q3.2 documented that 83% of findings clustered in retrieval+decay categories. The concentration problem is a **category selection** issue — the hypothesis generator selects question topics from its training distribution, not from a random draw over the full BrickLayer failure taxonomy. Temperature controls the sharpness of that sampling distribution, but:

1. At T≤0.50: the generator's most probable next-token sequences dominate, which in practice are variations on already-explored failure modes. The 10% uniqueness uplift in the model may overstate the actual category-diversification effect.
2. At T>0.50: malformed question rate increases faster than the diversity benefit, as confirmed by the -0.107 yield loss at T=0.70.
3. The resolution floor (1/28 ≈ 0.036) means even a real +10% uniqueness improvement is below the detectable threshold for this campaign size.

The **HHI sentinel** (Q6.4) remains the appropriate mechanism for directly detecting and reacting to category concentration, rather than attempting to prevent it via temperature adjustment.
