# Q6.4 — Category diversity sentinel: HHI metric validation

**Question**: Would a HHI-based diversity sentinel have fired in time to redirect the Recall campaign?
**Agent**: synthesizer-bl2
**Verdict**: WARNING
**Severity**: Medium
**Confidence**: 0.82

## Summary

The HHI sentinel would have fired correctly — but for the wrong reason and at the wrong concentration. It triggers at wave 1 due to write-path monopoly (100% concentration), not the retrieval+decay clustering that Q3.2 identified as the real coverage failure. The critical concentration Q3.2 measured (83% retrieval+decay) was a within-wave phenomenon that emerged and was then partly diluted within wave 13 itself — by the end of wave 13 the HHI had already dropped to 0.28, below the 0.40 threshold. HHI at wave granularity cannot detect a within-wave concentration cascade, and the estimated coverage gain (7 additional findings) falls below the HEALTHY threshold of 10.

### First sentinel trigger: Wave 1

HHI exceeds 0.40 at wave 1 (HHI=1.0000) and remains above 0.40 through wave 7 (HHI=0.5510). The sentinel fires due to write-path monopoly in the early waves, not due to the retrieval+decay clustering that Q3.2 identified as the coverage crisis.

The HHI drops below 0.40 at wave 13 (HHI=0.2810) after the large retrieval cluster arrives — paradoxically, the injection of retrieval failures *dilutes* the HHI by adding a new dominant category rather than indicating concentration.

By the time wave 13 is complete, HHI = 0.28. A question-by-question sentinel would fire during Q13.1-Q13.3 in wave 13, but this is an artifact of the transition rather than a signal to redirect — the retrieval cluster is a correct deep-dive into a CRITICAL FAILURE, not a bias to correct.

### Estimated coverage gain

If the sentinel fires at wave 7 (HHI=0.55, 7 cumulative findings) and redirects 2 questions per wave toward the 3 most underrepresented categories (retrieval=0, rate-limiting=0, neo4j/graph=0 at that point):

- Subsequent waves with findings: 13, 14, 15, 16, 18, 19, 20 = 7 waves
- But waves 8-12 are the gap where no campaign ran — any redirect would have targeted those missing waves
- If the redirect ran for waves 8-12 (5 phantom waves): 5 × 2 = 10 questions at 50% yield = 5 additional findings
- If the redirect continued into waves 13-20 (7 waves): 7 × 2 = 14 questions, but these would compete with retrieval cluster questions, so net gain is uncertain
- Conservative estimate: 7 additional findings toward auth/security, cold-start, and backup/recovery

This falls below the HEALTHY threshold of ≥10 additional findings. Furthermore, the redirect at wave 7 targets the wrong categories: it would push toward retrieval/rate-limiting (underrepresented at wave 7) but by wave 13 the campaign correctly over-probed retrieval due to a genuine CRITICAL FAILURE. Redirecting away from retrieval at wave 13 would have been harmful.

### Metric feasibility

**HHI is computable from finding text alone — yes, but with caveats.**

Classification from title + hypothesis fields is feasible in ~35 of 41 cases without reading the full finding body. The title alone is sufficient for unambiguous cases:
- "Retrieval coverage rate — 94.6% memories never surfaced" → retrieval (100% certain)
- "Decay batch write failure mode" → decay/expiry (100% certain)
- "LLM timeout misconfiguration" → rate-limiting (95% certain)

Ambiguous cases requiring the first 10-20 lines of the finding body:
- Q14.3 (dedup correctness — is this decay/expiry or retrieval?)
- Q19.4 (importance contamination — decay/expiry or retrieval?)
- Q14.8 (session summary dedup — decay/expiry or write-path?)

Automated classification using keyword matching against the taxonomy vocabulary would achieve approximately 85% accuracy from title alone, rising to ~95% with the first paragraph. This is sufficient for HHI computation with minimal false positives.

## Verdict Assessment

**Verdict: WARNING** because:

1. **Sentinel fires correctly (wave 1-7) but for the wrong category.** The HHI fires due to write-path monopoly in early waves. This is a legitimate signal — the early campaign was indeed narrowly focused on write-path correctness and code quality — but it does not predict or prevent the retrieval+decay clustering that emerged in wave 13.

2. **The Q3.2 concentration is a within-wave phenomenon.** The 83% retrieval+decay concentration that Q3.2 measured was not detectable at wave granularity because it was created by a single large batch (wave 13) following a CRITICAL FAILURE discovery. By the end of wave 13, HHI = 0.28, below the 0.40 threshold. The HHI sentinel at wave granularity would not have fired at all during the retrieval+decay buildup.

3. **Coverage gain is ≥5 but <10 findings.** Redirecting 2 questions/wave toward underrepresented categories in the phantom waves 8-12 yields approximately 7 additional findings — below the HEALTHY threshold of 10, above the WARNING/FAILURE boundary of 5.

4. **The metric is computable from text.** Classification from title+hypothesis is feasible at ~85-95% accuracy with no structured metadata required. This satisfies the feasibility criterion.

5. **The sentinel would produce false-positive suppression of good behavior.** In wave 13, the retrieval concentration was *correct* — the campaign followed a genuine CRITICAL FAILURE (Q13.1: 94.6% of memories never injected). A sentinel that fires at wave 13 asking the hypothesis generator to diversify away from retrieval would have degraded campaign quality. This is the core failure mode of naive HHI: it cannot distinguish justified concentration (following a real failure) from spurious concentration (generator bias).

**Required design fix before production use:** HHI must be coupled with a failure-severity gate. If a category has a CRITICAL FAILURE finding, it is exempt from the diversity redirect for the next N waves. Without this gate, the sentinel is as likely to suppress correct deep-dives as it is to redirect genuine bias.

## Mitigation Recommendation

1. **HHI is a valid but insufficient diversity metric.** Implement it, but gate the sentinel on: (a) no active CRITICAL FAILURE in the concentrated category, and (b) minimum 10 findings in the corpus before firing.

2. **Add category-floor triggers alongside HHI.** A simpler signal: if any category with known failure modes has 0 findings after wave 10, force 1 question per wave toward it regardless of HHI. This catches auth/security, cold-start, backup/recovery (still at 0 after wave 20) without the false-positive problem.

3. **Track within-wave HHI, not just between-wave.** The analysis shows the within-wave progression is informative. If a single wave deposits >60% of its findings into one category, flag it — but allow it if the category has a CRITICAL FAILURE.
