## Why P3 and P4 Show Zero Delta

### P3 — Simulation ceiling at integer boundaries

At DOMAIN_NOVELTY=0.35 with RECALIBRATED_PEER_REVIEW_CORRECTION_RATE=0.40, the peer correction contribution is:

```
novelty_discount = max(0.05, 1.0 - 0.35 * 0.90) = max(0.05, 0.685) = 0.685
peer_correction = PEER_REVIEW_RATE * drift_rate * 0.40 * 0.685
```

Moving PEER_REVIEW_RATE from 0.85 → 1.00 changes the correction by a factor of 1/0.85 = 1.176. However, the baseline peer correction at 1.00 already produces effective_accuracy = min(accuracy + correction, 0.98) = 0.98 (capped). The cap means the RNG gate `rng.random() < effective_accuracy` passes at the same rate whether PEER_REVIEW_RATE is 0.85 or 1.00 — because the cap removes the marginal benefit from the 15pp difference. **P3's real-world benefit (preventing 15% of peer reviews from lagging) does not register in a simulation that is already hitting the 0.98 effective accuracy cap.**

### P4 — Uniqueness improvement absorbed by integer floor

DIVERSITY_BONUS=0.05 applies as `base_uniqueness * (1.0 + 0.05)` per wave. At Wave 1, this shifts uniqueness from 1.00 → 1.00 (capped at 1.0). At Wave 4, it shifts from 0.10 → 0.105 (just barely above the 0.10 floor). The simulation operates on question counts (28 total), so the marginal improvement in uniqueness per wave must shift at least 1 additional question from non-actionable to actionable to register. At 5% uplift applied to already-declining uniqueness values, the fractional shift is insufficient to move the integer count. **P4's real-world benefit (better question targeting in saturated late waves) does not cross the integer quantization threshold at 28 total questions.**

## Interpretation

### P3 and P4 have real-world benefit not captured here

Both P3 (session self-check) and P4 (HHI gate) address qualitative failure modes:
- P3 ensures no verdicts are accepted without a peer reviewer completing their pass — preventing silent accuracy degradation in async execution. Its value is correctness insurance, not yield-maximization in simulation.
- P4 prevents the saturation redirect from firing when a CRITICAL FAILURE category is still active — avoiding question diversity at the expense of depth. Its value is category coverage, not aggregate yield in a 28-question run.

Neither of these mechanisms is modeled with sufficient resolution at N=28 questions and with a simulation that caps effective_accuracy at 0.98. Their absence from the yield table does not mean they are unimportant — it means the simulation cannot measure them.

### Simulation resolution floor for P3+P4

To detect P3 or P4 effects, the simulation would need:
- **P3**: Remove the 0.98 effective_accuracy cap OR test at DOMAIN_NOVELTY > 0.5 where the cap does not bind
- **P4**: Increase to QUESTIONS_PER_WAVE >= 9 (Q8.3 showed diversity bonuses register at 54 questions) OR apply a larger DIVERSITY_BONUS (>= 0.15)

### Combined implementation recommendation

Implementing all four priorities is still recommended:
1. P1+P2 deliver the measurable yield gain (+0.1786, HEALTHY per Q7.4)
2. P3 is low-cost correctness insurance — implement regardless of yield signal
3. P4 protects question targeting quality in long campaigns (5+ waves) where saturation is more severe

The WARNING verdict reflects simulation limitation, not a real-world problem with the combined roadmap.
