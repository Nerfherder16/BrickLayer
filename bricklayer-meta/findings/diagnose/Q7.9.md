### Structural vs. Accuracy Inconsistency

The generalist proxy inconsistency is primarily **structural** (empty Evidence = unverifiable findings), not **accuracy** failures. This aligns with the runner contract hypothesis: a structured output schema requiring an Evidence section would have prevented 11/11 criterion (a) failures. The Gaussian model in Q6.7 assumed variance reduction in accuracy; the empirical data suggests the runner contract's primary benefit is actually **evidence completeness** rather than accuracy precision — a different mechanism that still produces real yield improvement.

### Confounds

1. **Different finding modes**: Specialist findings are Mode: diagnose (research); generalist proxy findings are Mode: agent (task execution). The structural difference is not purely a runner-contract effect — agent-task output format differs from research output format by design.

2. **Small generalist proxy sample**: Only 11 generalist proxy findings available (vs. 195 specialist). The corpus is heavily dominated by specialist work.

3. **No true generalist fallback**: The Recall campaign has no genuine generalist fallback findings (no agent routing to a non-specialist). The comparison is specialist-research vs. non-specialist-task, not the ideal specialist-research vs. generalist-research comparison.

4. **Perfect specialist score may be inflated**: The 15 specialist findings sampled are all from quantitative-analyst performing code audits with live data access. These findings are exceptionally well-structured. A sample including Q33.x–Q37.x (later waves with different structural format) might show slightly lower consistency.

### Implication for Q6.7 Delta Calibration

The +0.215 Gaussian upper bound from Q6.7 is now partially empirically grounded: specialist agents in the Recall campaign achieve ~49 percentage points higher internal consistency than the generalist proxy group. If consistency maps to actionability (consistent findings are more actionable), the yield benefit from enforcing structured output is real.

However, the Q6.7 Gaussian model measured variance reduction in binary accuracy (`gauss(eff_acc, 0.10) > 0.5` vs. `random() < 0.824`). The empirical finding identifies a different mechanism: **evidence completeness**, not accuracy precision. The true yield delta from a runner contract is likely in the WARNING range (+0.05 to +0.10) rather than the HEALTHY range (+0.215) — the Gaussian model remains an upper bound, but the empirical data confirms a real positive effect.
