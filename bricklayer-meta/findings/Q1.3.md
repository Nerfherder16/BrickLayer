# Finding: Q1.3 — Specialization floor: minimum agent coverage before yield collapses

**Question**: There is a minimum AGENT_SPECIALIZATION_RATIO below which campaign yield degrades faster than linear — a cliff below which generalist fallback accuracy is too low to recover from.
**Verdict**: FAILURE
**Severity**: High

## Summary

No specialization floor exists at nominal novelty (0.35). Even 0% specialist agents yields 0.750 (HEALTHY). The range from 0%→100% specialization only moves yield from 0.750 to 0.821 — an 8% delta. No cliff, no collapse.

## Evidence

Full sweep at DOMAIN_NOVELTY=0.35, PEER_REVIEW_RATE=1.0:

| Spec ratio | Yield | Verdict |
|------------|-------|---------|
| 0.0        | 0.750 | HEALTHY |
| 0.1        | 0.750 | HEALTHY |
| 0.2        | 0.750 | HEALTHY |
| 0.3        | 0.786 | HEALTHY |
| 0.5        | 0.786 | HEALTHY |
| 0.7        | 0.821 | HEALTHY |
| 1.0        | 0.821 | HEALTHY |

No WARNING or FAILURE reached across the full range. The "inflection points" at 0.3 and 0.7
are RNG quantization artifacts from the discrete 28-question sample, not genuine nonlinearity.

**Root cause**: With DOMAIN_NOVELTY=0.35, even the generalist baseline accuracy of 0.625
gets boosted by peer review correction to ~0.77 effective accuracy. This is above the threshold
where yield stays healthy across 4 waves. The model's specialization gap (0.875 vs 0.625 = 0.25)
is too narrow to produce catastrophic failure at nominal novelty levels.

**The cliff only appears when peer review and specialization both fail simultaneously** — i.e.,
high novelty (peer review discounted) + low specialization (generalist baseline only).
At DOMAIN_NOVELTY=0.35, peer review absorbs the specialization gap entirely.

**Second calibration issue identified**: The BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY (0.625) may be too high.
If real-world generalist agents actually perform at ~0.50 accuracy (coin flip on unfamiliar
questions), the cliff would appear around specialization_ratio=0.25 at nominal novelty.
This warrants validation against real Recall OVERRIDE rate data (→ Q2.1).

## Mitigation Recommendation

1. Lower BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY from 0.625 to 0.50 and re-run — this models generalists
   as having near-random accuracy on questions outside their domain, which is more realistic
   for specialist-designed research campaigns.
2. Combine with the Q1.2 peer_review recalibration — together they should produce a visible
   specialization floor at moderate novelty.
