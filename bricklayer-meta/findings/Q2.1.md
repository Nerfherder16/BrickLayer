# Finding: Q2.1 — OVERRIDE rate calibration: does constants.py match empirical Recall data?

**Question**: The constants.py values BASE_SPECIALIST_ACCURACY=0.875 and BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY=0.625 were derived assuming ~12.5% and ~37.5% OVERRIDE rates respectively. Do the actual Recall campaign findings contain OVERRIDE verdicts at these rates?
**Verdict**: FAILURE
**Severity**: High

## Summary

Empirical OVERRIDE rate: **0%** (0 OVERRIDEs in 14 peer-reviewed findings). The assumed rates of 12.5% and 37.5% are completely unvalidated. BASE_SPECIALIST_ACCURACY=0.875 and BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY=0.625 are not grounded in real data. The entire simulation calibration depends on constants with no empirical basis.

## Evidence

### Peer Review Coverage in Recall Campaign

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total findings in recall/findings/ | 208 |
| Findings with `## Peer Review` section | 14 (6.7%) |
| OVERRIDE verdicts | **0** (0.0%) |
| CONCERNS verdicts | 1 (7.1% of reviewed) |
| CONFIRMED verdicts | 13 (92.9% of reviewed) |

### Peer Review Verdicts by Wave

The 14 findings with peer review sections span a small fraction of the campaign. The peer review system was not consistently active across all 36 waves — most findings show no evidence of peer review having run.

### What This Means for constants.py

| Constant | Assumed Basis | Empirical Finding | Validity |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|----------|
| `BASE_SPECIALIST_ACCURACY = 0.875` | ~12.5% OVERRIDE rate | 0% OVERRIDE in 14 samples | **UNVALIDATED** |
| `BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY = 0.625` | ~37.5% OVERRIDE rate | 0% OVERRIDE in 14 samples | **UNVALIDATED** |
| `PEER_REVIEW_CORRECTION_RATE = 0.55` | Theoretical | No empirical basis | **ASSUMED** |

### Confounds

1. **Small sample**: Only 14 of 208 findings have peer review data. The peer review system may not have been running consistently — the `## Peer Review` section only appears in ~6.7% of findings.

2. **Selection bias**: The 14 reviewed findings may be atypical. Peer review might have been triggered manually or for specific question types, not uniformly.

3. **Naming mismatch**: Peer review verdicts in real findings use CONFIRMED/CONCERNS/OVERRIDE format, but the actual Recall peer review system uses different thresholds than modeled. An OVERRIDE in the real system may be rare by design (the threshold is a high bar), not because agents are never wrong.

4. **No generalist/specialist differentiation**: Real Recall doesn't track which questions went to specialist vs generalist agents, so the differential accuracy assumption cannot be directly validated.

## Root Cause

The constants were reverse-engineered from the autosearch architecture description, not from empirical measurement. The 12.5%/37.5% OVERRIDE rates were plausible assumptions, not measurements.

The real peer review system appears to produce very few OVERRIDEs — either because:
(a) The agents are actually performing well and need few corrections, OR
(b) The peer review system is rarely running (coverage gap), OR
(c) The OVERRIDE threshold is set high enough that CONCERNS without OVERRIDE is the typical dissenting verdict

## Impact on Simulation Validity

All Q1.x findings that depend on BASE_SPECIALIST_ACCURACY and BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY should be treated as **model behavior**, not **ground truth**. The simulation is internally consistent but not empirically calibrated.

Specifically:
- Q1.2 (novelty cliff): finding that peer review over-corrects at high novelty — the correction rate may be wrong in both directions
- Q1.3 (specialization floor): finding that no floor exists at nominal novelty — the generalist accuracy may be wrong

## Mitigation Recommendation

1. **Accept the calibration gap**: The simulation is a structural model, not a calibrated predictor. Findings are about architectural relationships, not absolute thresholds.

2. **Re-examine peer review coverage**: Count how many Recall waves have ANY peer review activity. If peer review was running consistently but producing CONFIRMED verdicts, that is signal (agents are performing well). If it was rarely running, the architecture has a different problem.

3. **Lower BASE_GENERALIST_ACCURACY to 0.50** (as Q1.3 recommends) to test the structural model's sensitivity. This does not require empirical validation — it's a stress test.

4. **Do not rebase the threshold verdicts**: The FAILURE verdict for this question reflects that the constants are unvalidated, not that the simulation is wrong. The simulation should continue to be used with the caveat that absolute yield numbers are not trustworthy, only relative comparisons.

## Open Follow-up Questions

- **Q2.1a**: How many waves in the Recall campaign had ANY peer review activity? Is the 6.7% peer review rate uniform across waves or concentrated in early/late waves?
- **Q2.1b**: What fraction of Recall findings show a revision or correction after initial posting (human override via dashboard)? This is the human analog to OVERRIDE and is the better ground-truth signal.
