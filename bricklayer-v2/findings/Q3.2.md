### Predict mode case for ADBP

ADBP has exactly what Predict mode needs: open WARNING findings with quantitative trajectories.

**Causal chain example (Q9.4)**:
```
Trigger: Q9.4 — Grocery burn rate 0.5%; steady-state pool 18.6x monthly mints
    ↓ slow burn rate means credits accumulate; no cap mechanism
Cascade: If grocery vendor network exits or reduces coverage
    ↓ $108.7M outstanding grocery credits must be honored at FMV with no redemption path
Impact: Platform insolvency or redemption freeze
Timeline: POSSIBLE — requires vendor network disruption; quantifiable
```

**Other open findings suitable for Predict**:
- Vendor network exit cascade (what if 20% of vendors exit in Wave 2?)
- Churn compound (employer churn + employee credits = orphaned outstanding)
- Regulatory reclassification (MSB boundary crossed if ADBP introduces a new flow)
- Solana network risk (transaction failure at NFC tap = employee denied benefit in real time)

### Why not Validate mode yet

Validate mode is for "does this design work before building?" ADBP's design includes unresolved economic risks (the vendor pool question). Validate should wait until the Predict analysis surfaces which design assumptions need revision. Sequence: Predict → Revise design assumptions (Research) → Validate → Build.

### Why Research is mostly complete

Research mode (stress-testing specific assumptions) is appropriate for assumptions that are binary: "Does the 2x amplification hold at scale?" Research waves 1-9 have covered the major assumption clusters. The remaining open question is NOT "does assumption X hold?" but "given assumption X holds, what breaks downstream?" — that's Predict, not Research.

## Verdict Rationale

HEALTHY: ADBP is clearly in the mid-research phase with quantitative open findings. Predict mode is the correct next step — it takes the economic model's open WARNING findings (vendor pool exposure, burn rate dynamics) and projects their downstream consequences, which directly informs the design decisions that Validate will later review.
