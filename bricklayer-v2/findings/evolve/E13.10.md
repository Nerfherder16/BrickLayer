### Code analysis: convergence mechanism

From `masonry/scripts/improve_agent.py`:

```python
if after_score > before_score:
    best_score = after_score  # keep
    verdict = "improved"
elif after_score == before_score:
    _restore_instructions(...)  # revert
    verdict = "no_change"
else:
    _restore_instructions(...)  # revert
    verdict = "reverted"
```

**Convergence guarantee**: The best-known instructions are always preserved. Score cannot decrease across loops (revert on regression). This is a greedy hill-climber, not gradient descent — it cannot recover from a local maximum.

**Oscillation mechanism**: LLM eval is stochastic. Each `run_eval` call samples `eval_size` records from the dataset and scores them. A genuine score of 0.556 (10/18) has ±0.055 1-sigma noise at `--eval-size 18`. This means:
- A "real" improvement of 1 example (0.056 delta) may appear as no-change or regression ≈30% of the time
- A "real" regression of 1 example may appear as improvement ≈30% of the time
- Only improvements of ≥2 examples (0.111 delta) are statistically reliable
