## Finding 2: Double-Decay Rate Analysis

System decay parameters (from `/admin/tuning/parameters`):
- `decay_rate`: 0.01 (per run, base rate)
- Formula: `new_importance = importance * ((1 - effective_decay) ** hours_since_last_access)`
- `effective_decay = decay_rate * (1 - stability)` (for stability=0: effective = 0.01)

Single-decay (correct behavior) vs double-decay (current bug) per run:
- Single: applies once → `importance * (1 - 0.01)^hours`
- Double: applies twice → `importance * ((1 - 0.01)^hours)^2 = importance * (1 - 0.01)^(2×hours)`

Effective doubling of the decay rate:

| Metric | Single-decay | Double-decay | Ratio |
|--------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Time to reach 0.3 from initial 0.5 | 51 hours (2.1 days) | 26 hours (1.1 days) | **0.51×** |
| Time to reach floor 0.05 from initial 0.5 | 228 hours (9.5 days) | 113 hours (4.7 days) | **0.50×** |
| Expected importance at 7 days (init=0.5) | 0.0924 (floor) | 0.0500 (floor) | — |

Both regimes reach floor for typical initial importance within the first 5-10 days. The key damage window is the **first week**: under double-decay, memories reach < 0.3 in 1.1 days vs 2.1 days, and floor in 4.7 vs 9.5 days. This front-loads the hygiene pipeline by ~1 week.
