## Finding 3: Double-Decay vs Single-Decay — Archival Count Correction

**Key correction to Q27.3 inflation queue framing:**

Under either regime, any memory with access_count=0 reaches the 0.05 importance floor within 4.7 days (double) or 9.5 days (single) — well before the 30-day hygiene gate. By day 30, the memory is at floor regardless.

Calculation:
- Initial importance 1.0 (maximum), no access:
  - Single-decay (0.01/hr): floor at 228 hours = **9.5 days**
  - Double-decay (0.02/hr): floor at 113 hours = **4.7 days**
  - Both reach floor ~2 weeks BEFORE the 30-day gate fires
- Conclusion: no memory with access_count=0 can survive above 0.3 at day 30 under either regime

The double-decay bug's actual impact:
1. **Information quality loss occurs earlier** (floor at 4.7d vs 9.5d): memories are "dead" for 25 more days before archival under double-decay vs 20 under single-decay
2. **Retrieval quality degraded**: during days 5-10, memories have importance 0.05 (floor) under double-decay but ~0.2-0.4 under single-decay — they rank lower in search results
3. **Archival count is unchanged**: same 3,289 memories archived either way
