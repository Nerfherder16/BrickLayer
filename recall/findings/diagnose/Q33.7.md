## Verdict Threshold Application

**Threshold criteria:**
- FAILURE: Archival backlog > 200 floor-clamped memories older than 30d
- WARNING: Backlog 50–200 floor-clamped memories older than 30d
- HEALTHY: Backlog < 50 floor-clamped older than 30d (or hygiene fired and cleared it)
- INCONCLUSIVE: Export unavailable or created_at field not in schema

**Measured result:** 0 floor-clamped active memories older than 30 days. The Feb 14 cohort (the only candidates for the >30d bucket) has 0 floor-clamped active members. All active Feb 14 memories maintain importance 0.30–0.40 due to sustained access activity.

**Verdict: HEALTHY** — The Feb 14 archival boundary was crossed today, but the cohort that crossed it is in good health. Double-decay has NOT caused measurable importance degradation in the oldest active memories. The floor-clamped backlog (1,049) exists but is concentrated in mid-life cohorts (7–21 days old), not the 30d+ boundary group. The archival backlog meeting the >30d floor-clamped threshold is **0**, well below the 50-memory WARNING threshold.

**Caveat:** The 1,049 floor-clamped total (Q31.3) is a real operational concern — but it is a hygiene backlog problem, not a Feb 14 cohort degradation problem. The specific question of whether the oldest memories have been degraded by double-decay is answered: they have not, because frequent access preserved their importance.
