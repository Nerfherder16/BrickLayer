## Finding 3: Weekly Repair Cycle — Mismatches Are Self-Healing (With Lag)

The scheduled `run_reconcile()` worker (main.py:424-429) runs every **Sunday at 5:30am** and ALWAYS repairs mismatches:
```python

```

`run_reconcile()` in `reconcile.py:49-53` unconditionally calls:
```python

```
This sets neo4j importance = qdrant importance for ALL mismatched memories (including superseded), effectively zeroing out the weekly mismatch accumulation.

**Repair/accumulate cycle:**
- Sunday 5:30am: ~5,572 accumulated mismatches repaired in one batch
- Sunday 5:31am: consolidation resumes → new mismatches begin accumulating at ~796/day
- By next Sunday: ~5,572 new mismatches
- Cycle repeats indefinitely

**Impact on live system**: During the 6-day window between repairs, up to ~5,572 superseded memories have neo4j importance=0.0 (vs qdrant=original importance). Since `find_related()` in neo4j filters `superseded_by IS NULL`, these superseded nodes are not used in graph traversal. The retrieval impact is low — but the reconcile is masking the accumulation, making monitoring unreliable.
