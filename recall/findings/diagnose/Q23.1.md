The decay logic enforces a hard floor at 0.05. No active memory has been deleted or dropped below retrieval threshold despite 28 days of double-decay. The floor is the primary safety mechanism limiting catastrophic damage.

## Finding 5: Damage Model Validation

The crude double-decay model (2× hours_since in the formula) was tested against empirical data:

| Age Group | Actual mean | Expected (single) | Actual/Expected |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1-3 days | 0.3316 | 0.3599 | 92.1% |
| 3-7 days | 0.2072 | 0.2476 | 83.7% (mean) |
| 7-14 days | 0.1361 | 0.0410 | 332% (floor dominates) |
| >14 days | 0.2250 | 0.0078 | 2885% (floor dominates) |

For 7-14d and >14d groups, actual importance is far above the single-decay prediction because **the floor at 0.05 prevents decay below that value**. The expected_single model predicts near-zero for those ages, but the floor catches everything at 0.05.

For 1-3d and 3-7d memories (pre-floor zone), actual importance is **8-16% below expected single-decay values** — consistent with the double-decay model accelerating loss.

## Finding 6: Post-Outage Slot Structure Change (Context from Q22.9)

The double-decay was not constant:
- **Pre-outage** (before 2026-03-14T18:15): 10 entries per slot [system, 7×ghost, user_id=2, system] — full double-decay for all memories
- **Post-restart 00:16–06:15**: 8 entries [7×ghost, user_id=2] — **no system run**, single-decay only
- **Post-06:30**: ~2 entries [system, system] — double-decay resumed for system memories, no per-user

The actual damage may be slightly less than a pure double-decay model predicts because of intermittent single-decay periods. This partially explains why the 3-7d median (0.793) is above the double-decay prediction floor.

| Threshold | Criteria | Status |

| FAILURE | >20% of memories >3 days ago at importance <0.05; measured <50% of expected at any age point ≥3d | NOT MET (0% below 0.05; floor prevents; 3-7d measured at 83-91% of expected) |
| WARNING | 10-20% aged 3-7d below 0.05; measured importance 50-80% of expected | **PARTIALLY MET** — 0% below 0.05 but 11.8% at floor overall; median 3-7d ratio = 0.793 (below 80% HEALTHY threshold); 12 confirmed premature casualties |
| HEALTHY | <10% of 3-7d memories below 0.05; measured within 80% of expected | NOT FULLY MET (0% below 0.05 ✓, but median 3-7d at 0.793 < 0.80; 11.8% of corpus at floor overall) |

**Verdict: WARNING** — The 0.05 importance floor prevents catastrophic loss: no memories have been purged or dropped below retrieval threshold. However, the double-decay bug is causing measurable degradation: 12 memories are confirmed premature floor-hits (1.4 days early on average), the median 3-7d importance sits at 79.3% of expected (just below the 80% HEALTHY threshold), and approximately 217 importance units have been stripped from the non-floored corpus. Over 28 days with ~168 double-decay slots, the effective lifespan of every memory has been compressed to ~50% of design intent. The floor is the corpus's primary defense — without it, the double-decay would have driven most memories to zero. Fix priority: Tier 0 (confirmed in Wave 22 synthesis), but the damage footprint is smaller than the Q22.1 FAILURE verdict alone implied.
