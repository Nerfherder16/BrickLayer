## Finding 2: Importance-Units Stolen by Double-Decay

For each active memory with age T days and initial importance I:
- Single-decay expected: `max(I × 0.9228^(4T), 0.05)`
- Damage = max(0, expected_single - actual_importance)
- Sum across all 5,975 active memories: **179.5 importance-units stolen**
- Average per active memory: **0.030 units** (6% of a typical 0.5 initial importance)

This estimate uses the approximation that actual importance ≈ double-decay expected; it understates damage for memories that have been partially boosted by access/reinforcement.

## Finding 3: 3-7d Cohort Ratio Methodology Note

The median imp/expected_single = 1.2988 (same as Q25.1) does NOT indicate absence of double-decay damage. The formula `importance / (initial × decay^(age_slots))` does not account for:
1. **Stability-adjusted decay**: High-stability memories decay slower than the 0.9228 base factor. The cohort median being above 1.0 reflects that high-stability memories dominate the 3-7d cohort.
2. **Consolidation boosts**: Merged memories receive +0.1 importance boost, creating above-baseline values.
3. **Recent memories**: Memories at age=3d have had fewer decay cycles; the damage is smaller in absolute terms than for older cohorts.

The floor-clamped count (673) is the most reliable direct damage metric — it requires no decay model assumptions.

## Finding 4: Hygiene Inflation from Double-Decay

When the hygiene cron runs on 2026-03-17, it will archive memories with:
- `importance < 0.3`
- `access_count = 0`
- `age > 30 days`

Under single-decay (0.9228/slot) for a 30-day memory starting at importance=0.5:
- Expected single-decay importance = 0.5 × 0.9228^120 = 0.5 × 0.00009 ≈ 0.05 (floor)

Under double-decay (0.9228²/slot = 0.8516/slot):
- Expected double-decay importance = 0.5 × 0.8516^120 = effectively 0.05 floor

At 30+ days under either decay model, essentially all low-initial-importance memories will be at the floor. So the first hygiene batch size will be driven by the access_count=0 criterion rather than the importance threshold — and the access_count criterion is the same under both decay models. The double-decay does NOT materially inflate the first batch size in this specific scenario because all 30-day-old memories are near the floor regardless of decay rate (the floor at 0.05 is reached well before 30 days under both models).

**Revised assessment**: The first hygiene batch will NOT be significantly inflated by double-decay. The inflation concern is more relevant for memories in the 7-20 day band that would approach 0.3 threshold under single-decay but not under double-decay.
