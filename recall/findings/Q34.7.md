## Summary

Natural recovery is not viable. **644 premature casualties** — memories that should still be above floor under correct single-decay but are currently clamped at 0.05 — cannot self-rescue. The access boost mechanism (+0.02 per retrieval) is mathematically insufficient given the poverty trap: floor-clamped memories are too weak to surface in retrieval, so they never receive the access events needed to boost them. **Bulk re-score is mandatory.** Without it, the 644 premature casualties will be hygiene-deleted within approximately 16 days.

## Poverty Trap Analysis

### Why natural recovery fails

**Step 1 — Retrieval probability at floor:**
- Floor importance: 0.05; corpus median: 0.150
- Importance contributes directly to retrieval scoring (see `retrieval.py:961`: `sibling_boost * memory.importance`)
- Floor memories have 1/3 the importance weight of average memories
- To surface, a floor memory must be **3× more semantically relevant** than an average memory to compensate
- 99.0% of floor-clamped memories have access_count=0 — they were never retrieved even when they were created; they decayed before ever being found

**Step 2 — Access boost formula (retrieval.py:1540–1542):**
```python
if hours_since >= 1.0:
    importance = min(1.0, importance + 0.02)
```
Each retrieval adds exactly **+0.02** importance (once per hour cap). There is no formula restoring to `initial_importance`. Recovery is purely additive at +0.02 per access.

**Step 3 — Escape velocity math:**
- Decay at floor: `0.05 × 0.04 = −0.002` per 6-hour period = **−0.008/day**
- Boost per daily access (optimistic): **+0.020/day**
- Net gain per day with daily access: +0.020 − 0.008 = **+0.012/day**
- Days to reach corpus median (0.150) from 0.050: **(0.150 − 0.050) / 0.012 = 8.3 days of daily access**

This would be achievable — if floor memories were being accessed daily. They are not. With importance=0.05 and access_count=0, they rank in the bottom 17.3% of the corpus. The probability of daily retrieval is effectively zero.

**Step 4 — Hygiene timer:**
The hygiene worker (`workers/hygiene.py`) deletes memories where:
- `access_count == 0` AND `importance < 0.3` AND `age > 30 days`

Floor-clamped premature casualties have median age 13.4 days → **~16.6 days remaining** before the hygiene window opens. Natural recovery requires ~8.3 days of daily access. Since access probability is near zero, these memories will be hygiene-deleted before natural recovery can occur.

## Required Action: Bulk Re-Score

The `POST /admin/rehabilitate-importance` endpoint already exists and targets `importance < 0.05` memories. However, the premature casualties are clamped at exactly **0.050** (the floor value), not below it. The endpoint's filter `importance < 0.05` may miss them by one decimal place — verify the endpoint uses `importance <= 0.05` or adjust the filter.

Re-score formula should apply single-decay from initial_importance:

```
new_importance = max(initial_importance × 0.96^(age_hours/6), 0.051)
```

This restores each memory to where it would be under correct single-decay, rather than a flat boost that could over-inflate legitimately old memories.

**Priority:** High. The 16-day hygiene window is the hard deadline.
