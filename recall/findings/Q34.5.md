## Question

What is the importance distribution of the 975 integer-0 (never-decayed) active memories? Are there zombie high-importance memories causing retrieval pollution?

---

## Method

Queried Qdrant directly via SSH on the CasaOS host (192.168.50.19, port 6333), scrolling the `recall_memories` collection with filter `user_id == 0 (integer) AND superseded_by IS NULL` in 4 batches of 250. Retrieved payload fields: `importance`, `access_count`, `created_at`, `source`, `domain`.

Also collected the null-`user_id` active pool (5034 points, double-decayed cohort) for comparison, and the system-wide importance distribution from `/admin/health/distributions`.

## Analysis

The retrieval pollution concern is **not realized**. The int-0 pool is dominated by low-importance (mean 0.185), never-accessed (92%) memories that were assigned low scores at creation and have never been reinforced. The absence of decay is irrelevant in practice: their importance was too low to trigger meaningful retrieval pressure in the first place.

The 18 memories with importance > 0.5 are all recent (created within 13 days), suggesting they are actively-used working memories from current sessions that happen to carry the integer-0 user_id — they haven't aged enough to decay regardless, and 9 of the 18 have non-zero access counts confirming active use.

The null pool (double-decayed by the decay bug) actually has *higher* mean importance (0.257) than the int-0 pool (0.185), which means the decay system is disproportionately penalizing the null pool rather than the int-0 pool. This reinforces Q33.2b's finding: the real damage from the decay bug is to the null pool, not the int-0 pool.

## Verdict: HEALTHY

- Zombie count (strict definition): **0**
- Memories with imp > 0.5: **18** — all recent, well below the 200-memory FAILURE threshold
- Int-0 mean importance (0.185) is **below** the null pool mean (0.257) — no upward importance inflation
- 92% never accessed — confirms these are dormant low-value entries, not active retrieval contaminants
- No retrieval pollution from this cohort is detectable

### Risk Flag (Minor)

The infrastructure memory (imp=0.950, created 2026-03-02, acc=0) will cross the 14-day zombie threshold within ~1 day. If the integer-0 decay bug is not fixed, this single memory will qualify as a zombie. At one memory, this is noise — not a system risk.

---

## Root Cause Reminder

The int-0 never-decay bug (Q33.2b) is real and must be fixed, but its *importance distribution consequence* is benign. The int-0 pool's low intrinsic importance means the missing decay cycles have had no measurable retrieval impact. The fix is still warranted for correctness and to prevent future accumulation as higher-importance memories age into this cohort.
