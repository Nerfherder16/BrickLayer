# Finding: Q25.6 — ARQ anomaly is isolated catch-up event from Q22.9 outage; HEALTHY; no recurring burst pattern; 0.1–0.2 min pairs are normal double-decay; Q22.1 2× model remains accurate; Mar 15T06 delayed slot characterized

**Question**: ARQ worker restart anomaly characterization — what caused the Mar 15 timing anomaly and is it a recurring pattern?
**Verdict**: HEALTHY — The Mar 15 timing anomaly is an isolated catch-up event from the Q22.9 outage slot. The 200-entry audit history (22 complete 6h slots) shows no recurring ARQ restart pattern. The 20 "burst events" detected by the raw burst analysis are all normal double-decay pairs (11–13 second gaps between the two same-slot full-corpus runs). The Q22.1 double-decay characterization (2× factor per slot) remains the complete and accurate model of decay behavior.
**Severity**: HEALTHY — ARQ is stable; the anomaly is fully explained and isolated.

## Finding 2: Mar 15T00 Anomaly — Q22.9 Outage Catch-Up

The Q22.9 ARQ outage was documented at 2026-03-14T18:15–00:01. The expected 00:15:xx slot was missed during the outage. When ARQ restarted at ~00:01, a single catch-up run fired immediately:
- Catch-up ran at 00:01:20 (14 minutes before the scheduled 00:15:xx)
- Only 1 run fired (not 2) — the double-decay is asymmetric; the catch-up executed a single system run
- The ghost user runs (7 entries at 00:16) then ran at the normal 00:16:xx window for per-user jobs

This is a direct repeat of the Q22.9 catch-up pattern (single run at 00:01 after restart), not a new failure mode.

---

## Finding 3: Mar 15T06 Anomaly — Delayed Start After Restart

The 06:15 scheduled slot ran at 06:45 instead (30 minutes late):
- First full-corpus run: 06:45:09
- Second full-corpus run: 06:49:28 (gap: 4m 19s vs normal 11–13s)

The wide gap between the two runs (4.3 min vs normal 13 seconds) is unexplained but benign — both runs processed the same proc=5,936 active corpus. The most likely cause: ARQ was still initializing from the overnight restart; the first run was queued immediately and the second was delayed by post-restart job queue settling. This does not represent a triple-decay or additional damage beyond the normal 2× factor.

---

## Finding 4: Burst Analysis Classification

Raw burst detection (< 60-minute gap) found 20 "burst events." Classification:
- 18/20 are normal double-decay pairs (11–13 second gaps, systematic across all slots)
- 1/20 is the Mar 15T06 anomaly (4.3 minute gap — both runs from same slot)
- 1/20 is at the boundary of the 200-entry window (2026-03-10T00, only 1 full-corpus visible)

**No additional burst events outside normal double-decay**: There is no history of 3+ full-corpus runs in a single slot in the observable window.
