## Summary

Strategy D multiplies write volume by **5x** relative to Recall 1.0's blob model. At Tim's normal usage (2 sessions/day, 40 Write/Edit events/session), the 90-day corpus reaches **42,390 vectors** — about 2x the 22K assumption from prior analysis. ## Verdict

**PROMISING.** The 22K corpus assumption from prior analysis is outdated — Strategy D produces ~42K vectors at 90 days (normal usage), not 22K. The HNSW 500ms SLA holds at all realistic corpus sizes through 500K+ vectors. The chunking multiplier (5x) is a write-side concern, not a retrieval-side risk.
