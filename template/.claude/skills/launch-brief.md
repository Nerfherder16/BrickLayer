# /launch-brief — Product Launch Brief

You produce a launch brief that any team member can pick up and execute. You do not produce vague strategy documents. Every section contains decisions, not options.

## Step 1 — Gather launch parameters

Ask or infer:
1. **What's launching**: Feature / Product / New pricing / Open source release / Partnership
2. **Launch date**: Hard date or flexible?
3. **Launch scope**: Soft launch (existing users) / Limited beta / Public launch
4. **Success metric**: What number defines launch success?
5. **Team**: Who's involved? (1-person vs team changes the plan)

## Launch Brief Structure

### 1. Launch Summary (one paragraph)
```
On [DATE], we are launching [WHAT] to [AUDIENCE SEGMENT].
Success = [SPECIFIC METRIC] within [TIMEFRAME].
This launch matters because [ONE-SENTENCE BUSINESS REASON].
```

| {segment 2} | {N} | {one-line message} | {channel} |

### 3. Launch Assets Checklist

**Must have (launch blocked without these):**
- [ ] Landing page / updated product page
- [ ] Launch announcement post (primary channel)
- [ ] Email to existing users/waitlist
- [ ] Demo video or screenshots
- [ ] Pricing page (if applicable)

**Should have:**
- [ ] Press release or blog post
- [ ] Social content calendar (day-of + 3-day follow-up)
- [ ] Community posts (Hacker News, Reddit, Discord, Slack groups)
- [ ] Launch video (Loom or professional)

**Nice to have:**
- [ ] Product Hunt listing
- [ ] G2 / Capterra profile update
- [ ] Partner/affiliate outreach

## Output

Deliver the complete brief with all sections filled in, ready to hand to any team member. Mark any section as `[NEEDS INPUT: {what's missing}]` if you lack the information to fill it in.
