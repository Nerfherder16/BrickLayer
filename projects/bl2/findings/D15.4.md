# Finding: D15.4 — agent_db.py unclassified verdict handling

**Question**: Does agent_db.py silently treat unclassified verdicts as failures?
**Verdict**: HEALTHY
**Severity**: Info
**Mode**: code_audit
**Operational Mode**: diagnose

## Analysis

`_compute_score()` (agent_db.py lines 113-121) has the structural vulnerability described: any verdict not in `_SUCCESS_VERDICTS` or `_PARTIAL_VERDICTS` contributes 0 credit to the numerator while still incrementing the denominator, silently depressing the score with no warning, log, or exception.

However, a full set-difference check of all 30 active BL 2.0 verdicts against the union of all three frozensets shows **zero unclassified verdicts**. All BL 2.0 verdicts emitted by runners are covered.

One phantom entry: `DONE` is in `_SUCCESS_VERDICTS` but no runner emits it — benign.

## Assessment

No current failure. The absence of a guard (assertion or set-difference check at module load) remains a **latent risk** for future verdict additions, but is not an actionable finding today.

**Verdict: HEALTHY** — no fix required at this time.
