## Summary

True rehabilitation requires the forward fix (Q10.3.2 rubric) to accumulate new high-scoring memories over time.

## Why Amnesty Was Insufficient

The amnesty floor is `max(current, initial * 0.5, 0.3)`. For a memory with `importance=0.25`:
- `max(0.25, 0.125, 0.3)` → boosted to **0.3**

But 0.3 is still in the 0.3–0.4 histogram bucket (below HEALTHY threshold). With ~3,200 memories in the 0.3–0.4 range post-amnesty, the mean stays compressed near 0.39.

A floor of 0.4 would have moved the needle — but amnesty is intentionally conservative to avoid artificially inflating genuinely low-value memories.

## Rehabilitate vs Amnesty

| Endpoint | Targets | Floor | Use case |
|----------|---------|-------|----------|
| `/admin/importance/rehabilitate` | importance < 0.05 | 0.1 | Near-zero memories, emergency rescue |
| `/admin/importance/amnesty` | durable below 0.4 | 0.3 | Bulk boost pre-rubric corpus |

Rehabilitate was NOT useful here (targets < 0.05 only; most corpus is 0.3–0.4).

## Forward Path

1. **Q10.3.2 rubric fix** (deployed): New signals score 0.5+ for technical domains — corpus mean will naturally rise as old low-scored memories decay and new signals accumulate.
2. **Time to HEALTHY**: Estimated 2–3 weeks of normal usage given the current decay rate (~1,835 decay events observed in Q10.1).
3. **No aggressive backfill**: Artificially boosting old memories risks surfacing stale content inappropriately. Natural decay + new signals is the correct path.

## Verdict Rationale

Amnesty was applied without errors and is a legitimate conservative remediation. Mean moved negligibly (0.392 → 0.393). The corpus remains below HEALTHY threshold (0.40) and will require natural recovery. WARNING — not FAILURE because the forward fix is deployed and the trajectory is correct.
