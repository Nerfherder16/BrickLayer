**Question ID**: R13.1
**Status**: DONE
**Verdict**: WARNING
**Severity**: Medium
**Confidence**: 0.97

## Summary

`score_all_agents.py` runs without errors and produces a valid `scored_all.jsonl` with 64 entries from 10 agents. However, masonry's own research-campaign findings (fix-implementer, diagnose-analyst, design-reviewer, benchmark-engineer) do not appear. `score_findings.py` explicitly excludes the `masonry/` directory from its scan. The 5 research-analyst entries in the output are from ADBP findings — not masonry self-research. Masonry's high-confidence findings (0.88–1.00) would score 90+ points under the rubric but are never fed into the `scored_all.jsonl` training pipeline.

## Root Cause

`scripts/score_findings.py:282` explicitly excludes `masonry/` from the project subdirectory scan:

```python
for child in base_dir.iterdir():
    if child.is_dir() and child.name not in ("findings", "masonry", ".git"):
        _collect(child / "findings")
```

This is a **deliberate design decision**: masonry is the framework/infrastructure, not a research project being tested. The self-research findings are "meta" — using them as training data for agents that generated them would create a feedback loop.

However, the exclusion also means 90+ findings from masonry's research campaign (with explicit confidence 0.88–1.00, scoring ~90–100 under the rubric) never reach DSPy training. This is the same gap identified in R13.4 — the two scoring pipelines (`score_all_agents.py → scored_all.jsonl` vs. `build_dataset() → training_extractor`) serve different audiences.

## Verdict

**WARNING** — `score_all_agents.py` works correctly. The masonry exclusion is intentional. But the consequence is that masonry's research agents have zero training examples in `scored_all.jsonl`. The `build_dataset()` / `training_extractor.py` path (used by `optimizer.py`) is the correct path for masonry agent optimization. The two pipelines are parallel — `scored_all.jsonl` is for cross-project fleet training; `build_dataset()` is for masonry-scoped optimization.
