The guard prints a warning but does not block optimization. The optimizer proceeds with a low-tier prompt block of `(none)` — no negative contrast for the failure modes (bot-commit detection, revert classification, quality_score calibration) that the 3 failing eval records represent.

### E5: synthetic_negative records not in eval window

`scored_all.jsonl` lines 440-444: 5 synthetic_negative records appear before line 528. With eval_size=30, they are outside the held-out window. `_EXCLUDED_SOURCES` also blocks them from the training pool (F-w41.4). No impact on eval or training.

### E6: Eval asymmetry — synthetic_negative present in eval pool

`eval_agent.py._load_records()` does NOT apply `_EXCLUDED_SOURCES`. The 5 synthetic_negative records can appear in the eval pool if they fall within the last 30 records. They do not (lines 440-444 vs. last 30), so this is not currently affecting the before_score.

## Analysis

Before_score=0.90 is interpretable and the improvement gate is satisfiable. The structural problem is different: the optimization loop will run but produce no signal on the failure modes, because the 5 organic_low records (which represent the 3 currently-failing eval cases) are all held-out and excluded from the training pool.

The optimizer will receive 374+ high-quality examples and 0 low-quality examples. It will optimize against the positive pattern only — equivalent to running with no negative contrast. This is the same blind spot that existed before F-w41.4, just mediated through the exclusion mechanism instead of mislabeling.

**Root cause**: organic_low records appended at end of file → always inside `records[-eval_size:]` → always excluded from training pool. Fix: interleave organic_low records throughout the file, or change eval selection to random sampling rather than last-N.

## Verdict Reasoning
