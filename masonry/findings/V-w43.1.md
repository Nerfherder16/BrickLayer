# Finding: V-w43.1 — Research-Analyst Optimization Hard-Blocks with F-w43.1 Alone; F-w43.2 Required

**Question**: End-to-end dry-run validation — can research-analyst optimization complete after F-w43.x fixes?
**Agent**: design-reviewer
**Verdict**: FAILURE (conditional — PASS requires both F-w43.1 AND F-w43.2)
**Severity**: High

## Evidence

### E1: Score distribution for all 36 research-analyst records

| Tier | Score range | Count |
|------|-------------|-------|
| High (score ≥ 75) | 75-90 (Waves 9-12) | 27 |
| Mid (50-74) | 60-70 (Waves 7-8) | 9 |
| Low (score < 50) | — | **0** |

**Zero low-tier records exist for research-analyst.** All 36 records score ≥ 60.

### E2: Last-N held-out creates temporal skew (F-w43.1 only, before F-w43.2)

Corpus is chronologically ordered — early waves (7-8) score 60-70, later waves (9-12) score 75-90. `records[-26:]` (last 26 with corpus cap) selects all Wave 9-12 records (scores 75-90). Training pool = first 10 = Wave 7-8 records (scores 60-65, except 2 at 75-80).

Training pool tiers with last-N:
- High (≥75): **2 records**
- Low (<50): **0 records**
- Combined: **2 < `_MIN_TIER_RECORDS=5` → hard block fires (return code 1)**

F-w43.1 alone does not unblock optimization.

### E3: Random sampling (F-w43.2) resolves the hard block

With random sampling, training pool of 10 expected composition:
- High (≥75): ~(27/36) × 10 ≈ 7.5 records
- Low (<50): 0 (structural — none exist)
- Combined: ~7-8 ≥ 5 → **hard block does NOT fire**

Per-tier warnings: low-tier warning fires (0 < 5) but is warn-and-continue. High-tier warning: 7-8 ≥ 5 → no warning.

### E4: Structural low-tier gap — zero records below score 50

Even with F-w43.2 applied, the `low_block` in `_build_prompt()` renders as `"(none)"`. The optimization prompt's "Low-Quality Outputs" section is empty. The optimizer receives only positive examples — no negative contrast. This is a corpus quality gap, not a wiring defect:
- All research-analyst findings in scored_all.jsonl scored ≥60 (Wave 7+ records reflect post-improvement quality)
- Generating genuine low-quality research-analyst outputs would require deliberately bad findings

### E5: F-w43.2 implemented and fixes the hard block

F-w43.2 replaced last-N with `random.seed(42) + random.sample()`. With this change:
- Training pool ~7-8 high-tier records (combined > 5)
- Hard block does not fire
- Optimization proceeds with high-quality positive examples and empty negative block

## Analysis

The design claim — "can research-analyst optimization complete after F-w43.x fixes?" — is **conditionally true**: requires both F-w43.1 (corpus cap) AND F-w43.2 (random sampling). With both applied, optimization completes but with a structural limitation: no negative contrast examples exist for this agent.

**Structural low-tier gap requires a human decision:**

Option A — Accept one-sided optimization: run with high-tier examples only. The optimizer improves toward positive patterns; it cannot learn what to avoid. Risk: format optimization without correctness signal.

Option B — Lower the low-tier threshold: change `score < 50` to `score < 70` for research-analyst. This promotes the 9 mid-tier records (scores 60-70) to the low tier. These are older Wave 7-8 findings with lower quality — they represent what the agent used to produce before improvement. The optimizer would then have 9 negative examples showing earlier patterns.

Option B is a threshold policy change — requires human judgment on what "low quality" means for this agent.

## Verdict Reasoning

FAILURE (before F-w43.2) / WARNING (after both fixes): F-w43.1 alone hard-blocks at `optimize_with_claude.py` line 301 — combined=2 < MIN_TIER=5. F-w43.2 resolves the hard block by distributing records randomly, yielding ~7-8 high-tier training records. With both fixes applied, optimization runs but produces one-sided prompts (no low-tier examples). The zero-low-tier structural gap requires a human decision before research-analyst optimization produces meaningful results.
